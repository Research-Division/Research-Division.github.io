Imposed Tariffs & Effects: (CHN)

Implemented vs. Original Tariffs by Section

This chart shows a comparison between the original tariff rates and the current imposed tariff rates by HS section. Missing bars indicate no tariff or no import data for that section.

Key observation: Sections with the largest tariff increases are likely to experience the most significant trade effects. The impact depends on both the magnitude of the tariff change and the importance of that section in bilateral trade.

Tariff Effects Breakdown

This treemap visualization shows the breakdown of tariff effects across economic sectors. Each box represents a sector of the economy, with larger boxes indicating greater effects.

Use the following controls to explore the data:

  • Click on a sector to drill down and explore more detailed breakdowns
  • Use the tabs above the chart to switch between direct, indirect and total effects
  • Use the reset button to return to the top-level view

Direct effects occur when retailers increase the prices of imported goods sold to consumers in response to changes in tariffs on those imported items. The overall potential PCE price impact from the direct approach depends on the import share of various consumption categories, their respective spending weight in PCE, and the amount of the cost increase from higher tariffs that firms choose to pass through to prices.

Indirect effects capture how tariff-induced cost increases transmit through intermediate input relationships within the domestic economy. When tariffed imports serve as production inputs, they can potentially raise costs throughout interconnected supply chains. This tool assumes that firms embed tariff related cost increases directly in what they charge customers.

Total effects represent the sum of direct and indirect effects.

Values shown are for a household with annual income of $100,000. Go to the FAQ's to learn about how to interpret these results.
